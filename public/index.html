Why I'm the Programmer to Hire

My passion in life is to enjoy it to the fullest. Back in highschool I had a friend, Josh, who always told me to “Go big or Go home!”. I try to live my life by this saying, whether that be scuba diving in the Pacific ocean, playing ultimate frisbee with some friends, or writing a program. I try to make sure, no matter what I’m doing, it counts. Programming, for me, is like reading a book for an avid reader; I get lost in my code and enter a new reality. I get into this zone where it is me and the problem and the only way out is to solve the problem. Sometimes the pieces needed for solving the problem are hard to find and aren’t always exactly what I need, but I don’t give up and if there is a solution out there I will find it.

I understand the cost benefit curve and I know where to best spend my time. Looking at the big picture is one of my greatest strengths. Being able to take a large problems and break them up into smaller manageable pieces. Adapting my skills to different situations to achieve the best outcome. I strive to be the best and I’m always looking for new ways to improve myself. I’m great with people and understand social cues. Coming in handy because of the rarity of this trait in software engineers. I know my strengths and weakness, and I always play to my strengths. I am willing to admit when I am not the person for the job, but also the first to jump on a task when I know I am. I know many different programming languages and am always striving to learn more and become more proficient in the on I do know. My main focus is on web development, more specifically creating web apps and software that makes life more enjoyable for everyone, making the world a better place and allowing me to live life to the fullest.
